Regular Present-Tense Verbs · Definite & Indefinite Articles

📐 GRAMÁTICA 2 — Regular -AR, -ER, -IR Verbs

Remove the infinitive ending (-ar / -er / -ir), then add the corresponding personal ending.

hablar (-ar)
yohablo
túhablas
él/ellahabla
nosotroshablamos
ellos/Uds.hablan
comer (-er)
yocomo
túcomes
él/ellacome
nosotroscomemos
ellos/Uds.comen
vivir (-ir)
yovivo
túvives
él/ellavive
nosotrosvivimos
ellos/Uds.viven
💡 Pattern Tip: -ar and -er/-ir share the same yo ending (-o) and the same ellos/Uds. ending (-an vs -en). The nosotros form preserves the infinitive vowel: hablamos / comemos / vivimos.
More verbs — -ar: caminar, practicar, escuchar, estudiar  |  -er: beber, leer, comprender  |  -ir: escribir, recibir, abrir
📐 GRAMÁTICA 3 — Artículos y Sustantivos

All Spanish nouns have grammatical gender: masculine or feminine. Articles must agree in gender and number with the noun.

Definite (the) Indefinite (a / some)
Singular Mel libroun libro
Singular Fla claseuna clase
Plural Mlos librosunos libros
Plural Flas clasesunas clases
💡 Tip: Most nouns ending in -o are masculine; most ending in -a are feminine. Learn every noun WITH its article: el libro, la clase.
¡OJO! Some nouns ending in -e or a consonant can be either gender: el/la estudiante, el examen (M), la clase (F). When in doubt, look it up and add it to your vocabulary notebook with its article.
